Key Findings from the Report

  • The report identified the dominant species as Aspergillus/Penicillium, comprising 98–99% of all spores detected
  • The report identified Chaetomium in the massage room, described as a water damage indicator species
  • The report noted active hyphal fragments (described as live mould growth) across all indoor samples
  • The report noted a musty odour at the entrance, attributed to microbial volatile organic compounds (mVOCs)
Massage Room & Office CONDITION 3 The report classified these areas as Condition 3 under the ANSI/IICRC S520 standard
Entrance Area CONDITION 2 The report classified the entrance area as Condition 2 under the ANSI/IICRC S520 standard

The report recorded indoor humidity at 63.5% and attributed this to moisture retained in the building structure.

02

Structural Moisture Findings

The mould and moisture report identified the following structural moisture issues:

  • The report noted a large ornamental pond built directly against a below-ground wall with no adequate drainage
  • The report noted external ground level is higher than the internal floor level of the massage room
  • The report recorded an external wall moisture reading of 607 REL
  • The report noted paving installed directly against the wall with no drainage clearance
  • The report noted garden beds pressed against the building envelope
  • The report noted the subfloor drainage area was blocked with debris and moisture
